# PRODUCT.md — sharelinks

**register:** brand

## Product purpose
sharelinks gives people who generate lots of one-off HTML files (reports,
dashboards, quick-link pages, showcases) a single home: one gallery that lists
everything they've published, auto-grouped by theme, each piece reachable by its
own live URL. Publishing is fully headless via Surge.sh — an agent (Claude Code)
creates the account, publishes, and maintains the gallery from the CLI, no browser.

The **gallery page** is the one screen a user actually looks at and shares. It is
the product's face. It should feel like a place you're glad to land on, not a file
index. Design is part of the value here.

## Users
- Makers, analysts, and builders who spit out HTML reports constantly and lose
  track of them in folders.
- People who want a shareable link for a showcase without standing up hosting.
- The gallery owner returns often to browse; visitors arrive via a shared link.

## Voice / tone
Charming, crafted, a little playful, quietly confident. Not corporate, not
"dashboard." Three brand-voice words: **warm, hand-made, whimsical-but-precise.**

## Aesthetic direction
**Parisian pixel art.** The warmth and elegance of a Paris café — cream paper,
soft sky blue, brass, a touch of rouge, deep ink — rendered through the chunky,
limited-palette charm of 8/16-bit pixel art. Think a hand-built pixel poster of
Haussmann rooftops and an Eiffel Tower, not a SaaS dashboard. A day (cream) world
and a Paris-at-night (indigo, lit windows) world.

## Anti-references
- SaaS dashboards: hero-metric tiles, icon+heading+text card grids, gradient accents.
- Editorial-magazine reflex: display-serif italics, drop caps, broadsheet grids.
- Generic "developer tool" monospace-everything costume.
- Anything that reads "AI made that."

## Strategic principles
- The gallery is a destination, not a listing. Reward the glance.
- Theme grouping is the core IA — make groups feel like distinct little districts.
- Imagery is hand-built pixel illustration, never stock photos (photos clash).
- Fast and self-contained: one HTML file, minimal external requests, works offline
  after fonts cache. Never break the "just works" promise for decoration.
